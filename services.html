Desktop Publishing (DTP) is a method of creating printed materials which involves using a layout tool on a desktop computer to combine text and graphics. This step ensures that the translated and localized material retains the same structure, layout, and format as the original document while giving it a final polish. Several steps are involved in organizing the overall look and format of a translated and proofread product.

To design the layout of printed materials, desktop publishers utilize applications such as QuarkXPress and Adobe InDesign. A wide variety of printed materials, including books, periodicals, brochures, newspapers, and flyers, can be made using desktop publishing technologies. Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator are among the desktop publishers' go-to tools for making printable graphics. You may utilize even the most common and easy-to-understand word processors, like Microsoft Word, for basic desktop publishing tasks.

Graphic design, page layout (determining how everything fits on the page), printing, and typesetting (determining fonts and text arrangement) are all parts of a comprehensive desktop publishing process.
