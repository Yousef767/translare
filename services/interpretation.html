Consecutive interpretation

It is a mode of interpreting conducted in a two-stage process where one party speaks and pauses until the interpreter delivers the response to the other party. It’s typically utilized in one-on-one or small-group situations to enable people who speak two or more languages to communicate more effectively.

Normally ideal for

  • Small business meetings
  • Client-attorney meetings
  • Medical consultations

Simultaneous interpretation

It takes place over the speech as it’s taking place, usually with a delay of 30 seconds or less.

This type of interpretation is very challenging for the interpreter, who must be able to translate from one language into the target and convey that content to an audience while listening to a real-time speech.

Used at events where speakers’ responses are interpreted simultaneously

  • Conferences
  • multilingual business meetings
  • Events
  • Tours

Over-the-phone interpretation

Over-the-phone interpreting (OPI) service tends to be the most accessible option.

It is essentially a 3-way conference call, where the interpreter facilitates the discussion between the two parties speaking different languages.

Clients can receive either consecutive or simultaneous interpretations, depending on the project.

TRANSLARE provides over-the-phone interpretation services so you get on-demand multilingual interpreting support when and where you need it.

Typically needed for

  • Legal
  • Medical
  • Finance
  • Manufacturing

Remote video interpretation

Video Remote Interpretation (VRI) is the ideal format for professional real-time language access. VRI works by connecting an interpreter, who is typically at a call center equipped with a headset and video camera, with people who need assistance with communicating in various languages.
